# Labs Introduction

These labs will give the full experience of developing, building, deploying, managing and troubleshooting containers. Every lab contains several exercises to complete.

If you are unable to complete certain exercises, no problem. You can always complete the other labs.

## Labs

| #   | Lab Name                                                                             | Description                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
|------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1   | [Container Introduction](01-ContainerIntroduction/README.md)| In this lab during the exercises attendees will learn about the history of containers and get hands-on with  `chroot` and `cgroups`.|
| 2   | [Container Runtimes](02-ContainerRuntimes/README.md) | In this lab during the exercises attendees will learn about the container runtimes by experimenting with `ContainerD` and `RunC`.|
| 3   | [Docker CE](03-DockerCE/README.md) | In this lab during the exercises attendees will learn about how to setup an Docker CE Engine or a local Docker Desktop environment.|
| 4   | [Image Management](04-ImageManagement/README.md) | In this lab during the exercises attendees will learn about managing and maintaining images.|
| 5   | [Container Management](05-ContainerManagement/README.md) | In this lab during the exercises attendees will learn about managing and maintaining containers.|
| 6   | [Container Network](06-ContainerNetwork/README.md) | In this lab during the exercises attendees will learn about host, bridge driver and connecting multiple containers to a virtual network.|
| 7   | [Container Volumes](07-ContainerVolumes/README.md) | In this lab during the exercises attendees will learn about virtual volumes and how they can connect/consumed by containers.|
| 8   | [Container Development](08-ContainerDevelopment/README.md) | In this lab during the exercises attendees will learn about docker compose and how it connects all above container components together to a containerized solution.|

## Prerequisites

You may use any `Docker Community Edition (Linux) Engine` environment, but as minimum we advice the following prerequisites that are part of [Lab 3](03-DockerDesktop/README.md). For simplicity you can make use of a local `Docker Desktop`. Keep in mind this is for training purposes only!!

- 1 or more (virtual) CPUs available.
- At least 1G of Memory.
- Approx 5 GB available for storage.

** This training is developed and tested using Docker CE (29.3.0-1) release **
